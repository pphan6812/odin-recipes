Ramen

Description

Ramen is a Japanese dish that consists of Chinese-style noodles served in either meat or fish-based broth. It is often flavored with soy sauce or miso. The dish is finished with toppings such as sliced pork, dried seaweed, and green onions. Ramen became extremely popular after WW II due to its convenience in a different style of ramen known as instant ramen noodles. In today's time, ramen is enjoyed all around the globe, with many international variations spin on the iconic dish happening throughout the world.

Ingredients

    For the soup broth

  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 tsp of ginger (grated)
  • 1 shallot
  • 1 Tbsp toasted white sesame seeds
  • 1 Tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1/4 lb ground pork
  • 1 tsp doubanjiang (spicy chili bean paste)
  • 3 Tbsp miso
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 1 Tbsp sake
  • 4 cups chicken stock/broth
  • 1 tsp Diamond Crystal Kosher salt (to taste)
  • 1 tsp white pepper powder

    • For the Ramen Noodles

  • 2 servings fresh ramen noodles (10-12 oz (283-340 g) fresh noodles or 6.3 oz (180 g) dry ramen noodles)

    • For the Toppings

  • Homemade Chashu (Japanese Braised Pork Belly)
  • Spicy Bean Sprout Salad
  • Ramen Egg (Ajitsuke Tamago)
  • Frozen or Canned Corn
  • Nori (dried laver seaweed)
  • Green Onion/scallion
  • Shiraga Negi (julienned long green onions)

    • For the Condiments

Steps

    Preparation for Ingredients

  1. Prepare the ramen toppings ahead of time - a day in advance for the Homemade Chashu (Japanese Braised Pork Belly) and Ramen Egg (Ajitsuke Tamago).
  2. Gather all the ingredients
  3. Mince or press 2 cloves garlics and add to a small plate. Then, grate the ginger and add 1/2 tsp ginger (grated) to the plate.
  4. Mince 1 shallot and add to the plate. Set the ingredients aside.
  5. Grind 1 Tbsp toasted white sesame seeds, leaving some seeds unground for texture. Set aside.

    6. To make the Soup Broth

  6. Preheat a medium pot over medium-low heat. When it's hot, add 1 Tbsp toasted sesame oil. Then add the garlic, ginger, and shallot.
